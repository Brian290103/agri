Some FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What is sustainable agriculture?

Sustainable agriculture is a way of farming that aims to produce food while conserving resources, protecting the environment, and supporting economic viability.

Why is water conservation important in farming?

Water conservation is crucial in farming to ensure the sustainable use of water resources, reduce water costs, and improve crop yields.

What are the benefits of organic farming?

Organic farming promotes soil health, reduces pollution, supports biodiversity, and provides high-quality, nutritious food.

How can technology improve farming practices?

Technology can improve farming practices by optimizing resource use, enhancing productivity, and reducing environmental impact through precision farming, drones, and IoT devices.

What is climate-smart agriculture?

Climate-smart agriculture involves practices that increase productivity, enhance resilience to climate change, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

What is Community Supported Agriculture (CSA)?

Community Supported Agriculture (CSA) is a system where consumers directly support local farmers by subscribing to receive regular shares of the farm's produce.

What are the benefits of farm-to-table practices?

Farm-to-table practices provide fresh, locally sourced food, support local economies, and reduce the environmental impact of long-distance food transport.

How can renewable energy be used in farming?

Renewable energy, such as solar and wind power, can be used in farming to reduce energy costs, lower carbon footprints, and enhance sustainability.

What is agroforestry?

Agroforestry is the practice of integrating trees and shrubs into agricultural systems to improve land productivity, biodiversity, and sustainability.

Why is soil health important in agriculture?

Soil health is vital for agriculture as it influences crop growth, water retention, nutrient availability, and resistance to pests and diseases.
